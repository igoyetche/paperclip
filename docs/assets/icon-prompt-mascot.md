# Paperclip Icon — Full Mascot Prompt

## Prompt

Minimalist icon of a **sentient, anthropomorphized paperclip character** rendered in the **Vault Boy / Fallout mascot aesthetic** — 1950s atomic-age retro-futurist cartoon style, reminiscent of mid-century advertising mascots and SPECIAL-stat illustrations. A jaunty, cheerful paperclip with a personality, portrayed head-on or three-quarter view, embodying the Vault Boy spirit of confident retro optimism. This is the Paperclip companion to Paperboy; the two should read as sibling products with identical visual language, palette, and energetic pose.

**Character details:**
- The **main body** is a classic gem-shape / trombone paperclip — two elongated U-curves nested inside one another, forming a recognizable paperclip silhouette that reads instantly.
- **Pie-cut eyes** (Vault Boy's signature) — positioned on or near the outer loop of the paperclip, two circular eyes with a wedge-shaped slice missing from each. One eye is **winking closed** (a simple curved line), the other open and cheerful, conveying personality and warmth.
- A **wide confident grin** — a simple curved line forming a smile, placed below the eyes. Small rosy, rounded cheeks on either side (small filled circles).
- A **single small curl of hair** peeking out from the top of the paperclip (echoing Vault Boy's distinctive forelock) — optional, but adds character and ties visually to the Paperboy cap.
- Two **simple arms** — tube-like shapes with rounded shoulders, extending outward from the sides of the paperclip in a confident, presentational stance. Hands are classic cream four-fingered cartoon gloves or mitten-hands, gesturing openly as if to say "I'm here to organize and clip!"
- Optional **legs** — simple tube shapes extending downward from the bottom loop, standing firmly in a confident stance. Feet are simple rounded shapes in cream.

**Overall pose:** The paperclip stands front-and-center, confident and approachable, arms slightly extended outward in a "thumbs up" or welcoming gesture. The character radiates optimism and utility — a helpful, cheerful tool ready to organize your web clips. A subtle drop shadow adds gentle depth, making the character feel slightly lifted from the navy background.

**Style:** flat vector cartoon, bold confident outlines at consistent 3–4px stroke weight, mid-century advertising mascot energy. No gradients, no shading, no texture, no halftones, no third color. Every shape is a solid fill in one of the two palette colors. The feel is: cheerful retro mascot, 1950s print ad, Pip-Boy manual illustration.

**Palette (strict, three colors — exact match with Paperboy plus shadow depth):**
- Background: navy blue `#102C54`
- Paperclip character (outlines, fills, eyes, smile, cheeks, arms, legs, hands): cream `#F5E6BE`
- Drop shadow: navy blue `#102C54` at reduced opacity (~20–30% alpha) for subtle depth

The entire canvas is a solid navy blue fill. The paperclip character — body, loops, facial features, limbs, hands, outlines — is rendered entirely in cream. A **subtle drop shadow** in navy (20–30% opacity, offset ~8–12px down and right at the 1024px scale) sits behind the character, giving it depth and lift without overwhelming the flat retro aesthetic. The pie-cut slices of the eyes, the curved line of the winking eye, the smile, the rosy cheeks, and the interior loops of the paperclip (showing navy background through) all work together to give the character personality.

**Composition:** centered on a 1024×1024 square canvas, with generous navy blue negative space around the figure so it remains legible when scaled down to 48×48 and 32×32 extension icon sizes. The arms and legs should fit comfortably within the canvas without crowding the edges. The overall character footprint should have visual parity with Paperboy's icon — similar overall height and presence — so the two icons read as matched brand siblings.

**Mood:** cheerful, confident, retro, wholesome — a 1950s atomic-age utility mascot enthusiastically ready to clip, organize, and help. The cream-on-navy pairing feels like aged newsprint under streetlight. No text, no logo, no background scenery, no digital or tech motifs. Pure animated paperclip character rendered in the exact Vault-Boy style as Paperboy.

**Deliverable:** flat vector-style raster, sharp edges, no anti-aliased softness. The canvas is a solid navy blue square; output the full square-filled version.

## Design Rationale

**Why an anthropomorphic paperclip?**
- Creates a distinctive mascot character with personality and emotion — the paperclip itself *is* the character, not an object held by a human.
- At all icon sizes (128×128 down to 32×32), the mascot's face and pose are instantly recognizable and convey friendliness and utility.
- A paperclip character is more memorable and ownable than a faceless tool — it becomes the brand avatar.
- Maintains visual parity with Paperboy (same palette, same mascot-based aesthetic) while being visually distinct (a clip instead of a human).

**Why arms and legs?**
- Arms in a confident, welcoming pose echo Vault Boy's "thumbs up" energy and the "presenting" gesture of Paperboy.
- Legs ground the character and make it a full-bodied mascot rather than a floating object, increasing visual presence and character appeal.

**Why pie-cut eyes and a smile?**
- Stays true to the Vault Boy / Fallout aesthetic — pie-cut eyes are iconic to that style.
- A smile and rosy cheeks make the character warm and approachable, not sterile.
- Ensures instant emotional resonance when users see the extension icon.
